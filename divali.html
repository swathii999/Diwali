Happy Divali

Here's some information about Diwali, a popular Hindu festival that symbolizes the victory of good over evil: Name: The name Diwali comes from the Sanskrit word Deepawali, which means "rows of lights". When it's celebrated: Diwali is celebrated in the Hindu calendar month of Kartika, which is usually between mid-October and mid-November. It's celebrated on a no-moon night, also known as an amavasya. What it celebrates: Diwali commemorates the return of Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana to Ayodhya after Rama's 14-year exile. It also symbolizes the victory of light over darkness, good over evil, and knowledge over ignorance. How it's celebrated: Celebrations include: Lighting diyas (clay lamps) Placing lights on the walls and entrances of houses Worshipping Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth and prosperity Worshipping Lord Ganesha, the god of good luck Visiting, exchanging gifts, wearing new clothes, feasting, and feeding the poor Setting off fireworks Other traditions: On the first day of Diwali, known as Dhanteras, people clean their homes and buy small items of gold. On the fourth day of Diwali, people celebrate Govardhan Puja, which is a tradition to worship Govardhan Parvat (Mountain). On the fifth day of Diwali, known as Bhai Dooj, sisters pray for their brothers to have long and happy lives, and brothers give gifts to their sisters. Other religions: Diwali is also celebrated by Sikhs and Jains. Other countries Diwali is an official holiday in India
